Mastercard was the official Conmebol Copa América 2019 sponsor and the official sponsor of the Brazilian national squad. The brief was unambiguous: make the sponsorship stand for something only Mastercard could own. No logo placement, no generic football energy. A real idea.

The tension was real. Contactless payment technology was already widespread in Brazil — every POS terminal in the country carried the word _aproxime_ ("bring closer") as a functional instruction. But the behaviour hadn't yet become habit. Brazilians knew the word. They just hadn't made it theirs.

That gap was the opportunity.

---

## The Idea: Aproxime / Get Closer

_Aproxime_ is printed on every contactless POS terminal in Brazil. It's a technical instruction — tap your card, bring it closer. But in Portuguese, it carries a second register entirely. _Aproximar_ means to draw near, to close the distance between people. And that's exactly how Brazilians live football: together, warm, physically close.

The dual meaning became the campaign's spine. **Get Closer. Start Something Priceless.**

Functional technology meets emotional truth — both living in a single word that Mastercard already owned on every payment surface in the country.

## Outcome

Card-usage expectations were exceeded. The campaign became a global reference inside the Mastercard network. On the main brand tracker index, _Get Closer_ surpassed VISA for the first time — a new record that had never been achieved on the account.
